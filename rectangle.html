Rectangle Body Type

The rectangle body type is characterized by a straighter silhouette with minimal definition between waist, hips, and shoulders. Enhance your figure with dresses that add curves and create definition.

Creating Curves:

  • Wrap Dresses: The wrap style creates a defined waistline and cinches your figure without being too tight. Choose styles with a v-neckline to elongate your neck and torso. Explore
  • Empire Waist Dresses (with caution): While not the most flattering for all rectangular shapes, empire waist dresses with a slightly defined waist and a flowy skirt can add some visual curves. Explore
  • Fit and Flare Dresses: These dresses create an illusion of curves by hugging the bodice and then flaring out from the waist. This defines your waist and adds volume to your hips. Explore
  • Peplum Dresses (Strategically): Peplums positioned at the waist or slightly lower can add shape and create a more defined waistline. Explore

Adding Definition:

  • Belted Dresses: A belt cinches your waist and creates a more defined silhouette. Look for dresses with a built-in belt or add your own. Explore
  • Asymmetrical Dresses: These dresses add visual interest and can break up the straight lines of a rectangular body type. Explore
  • Dresses with Ruching or Draping: Gentle ruching or draping around the midsection or hips can add subtle curves and create a more feminine look. Explore

Materials:

  • Structured Fabrics with Some Drape: Opt for fabrics like ponte knit, scuba fabric, or tailored crepe that hold their shape while still offering some movement.
